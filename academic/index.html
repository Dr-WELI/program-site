Research focus

Science, culture, and multi-species understanding in practice

My research sits at the intersection of science and popular culture, with a focus on how music, audio-visual media, and storytelling shape public engagement with science and nature.

Grounded in ecology, animal behaviour, and evolutionary theory, my work explores social and behavioural systems across species, and the parallels between human and non-human societies as a basis for communication, education, and cultural production.

This research directly informs my creative practice through WildWooHoo, where I develop music, film, and educational projects that translate scientific knowledge into culturally resonant formats. The aim is to expand how science is experienced beyond academic contexts, reaching broader audiences through emotionally engaging and socially impactful work.

Current research

Science communication, culture, and public-facing knowledge

Current work includes collaborations with Dr Anna-Sophie Jürgens at CPAS, ANU, with focus on music and science communication, and on theatrical, camp, and speculative portrayals of science, including exaggerated scientists and alien characters.

Alongside ecological and behavioural research, this work expands into popular culture, humour, performance, and research-creation as forms of inquiry and engagement.
