Led by WELI

Dr Weliton Menário Costa (WELI) is a PhD-trained ecologist and transdisciplinary practitioner working across science, education, and creative performance. His facilitation blends nature-inspired systems thinking, music, and collective movement to unlock trust, connection, and creative thinking in groups. He is the international winner of Science Magazine’s Dance Your PhD, with work featured by 250+ global media outlets.

The experience is playful in delivery and rigorous in design: each workshop is scoped to audience pains (disconnection, low engagement, message complexity, career uncertainty) and built to produce practical outcomes in communication, confidence, and collaboration.

Service formats

The university experience arc is consistent and intentional: collective activation → communication/message session → workshop application → uplifting close students remember.

Activation

  • Short energisers for orientation/events
  • Creates safe social momentum quickly
  • Strong for transitions and large cohorts

Workshop

  • Communication and belonging focus
  • Identity, purpose, and participation
  • Ideal for mixed/interdisciplinary groups

Program

  • Multi-session cohort development
  • HDR, transition, and capability pathways
  • Co-designed to campus priorities

Outcome focus

  • ✔ Better peer connection across cohorts and disciplines
  • ✔ More confidence in communication and collaboration
  • ✔ Stronger engagement with identity and career direction
  • ✔ Inclusive, high-energy student experiences
  • ✔ Clearer translation of complex research for non-specialist audiences

Popular activation and workshop menu

  • Collective activations: guided dance, guided meditation, full-room karaoke, and group dynamics to reduce social friction fast
  • Message and talk modules: belonging, identity, purpose, and translating ideas for real audiences
  • Workshop studios: practical collaboration, communication confidence, and career-ready storytelling
  • WIL-strengthened options: selected sessions include guest professionals with real industry experience
  • Recurrent program pathways: single event, semester series, or multi-session cohort capability model
  • Uplifting close: a unifying finish that reinforces confidence, community, and action

Workshop themes (student + career relevance)

  • Nature-inspired creative thinking for research, teams, and leadership.
  • Connecting science and culture: turning complex ideas into audience-ready stories.
  • Building transdisciplinary careers with WIL-aligned employability pathways.
  • Dance Your Thesis: identifying the strongest message inside a research project.
  • Lyric-writing labs for cross-disciplinary communication and public engagement.
  • Science communication, popular culture, and humour through the Kangaroo Time case.
