Build belonging. Expand possibility.

University life works best when students feel connected — to each other, to their learning, and to possible futures. These sessions are designed to energise that connection.

This offer is student-first: helping students meet peers beyond their own cohort, develop creative and communication skills, explore how multiple interests can live together in one career, and feel more at home in the university experience.

Bring this to your students

Who this is for

  • 🎓 Student experience and student life teams
  • 🤝 Student associations, clubs, and cohort-building programs
  • 📘 HDR development and graduate research training
  • 🧩 Transdisciplinary, innovation, arts, music, and science schools
  • 🌿 University wellbeing and community events

What students get

These sessions help students engage beyond the usual academic frame. Instead of separating identity, interests, and career possibilities, the work invites students to bring them together.

  • ✔ Opportunities to meet and engage with students from other cohorts and study areas
  • ✔ Creative confidence, communication, and participation
  • ✔ New ways to think about career paths that combine multiple interests
  • ✔ A stronger sense of belonging and social connection

Formats

ACTIVATION

  • 5–20 minute energisers
  • Orientation, student events, conferences
  • Designed to reset the room and open connection

WORKSHOP

  • 60–120 minutes
  • Creative thinking, communication, identity, and careers
  • Ideal for mixed cohorts and interdisciplinary groups

PROGRAM

  • Multi-session delivery
  • Student development, HDR capability, cohort-building
  • Can be shaped with your student experience goals

Useful pathways

The same core format can be adapted differently depending on the audience.

  • General students: belonging, connection, confidence, and shared experience
  • Science students: creative science communication and wider career imagination
  • Transdisciplinary students: collaboration across fields and making multiple interests work together
  • Arts and music students: performance, expression, audience connection, and professional identity
  • HDR students: communication, presence, research identity, transferable capability, and career thinking beyond a single track

Typical flow

  • Collective warm-up: shared movement that lowers pressure and opens the room
  • Creative session: tools and prompts shaped to the students, context, and goals
  • Uplift and close: a memorable finish that helps the ideas land socially and emotionally
