Main
offer

For research institutes, cultural orgs, and funders

The flagship engagement. Built on the same practice that turned a PhD on kangaroo behaviour into Science Magazine's Dance Your PhD 2024 winning piece.

M.01 / Featured

Build a Kangaroo Time-style piece around your theme

A commissioned engagement where we take a research question, an institutional focus, or a campaign brief and turn it into a real piece of culture using the WildWooHoo studio process. The output is the deliverable. A song, a short film, a performance, a public moment, a piece of integrated media. Designed to reach audiences your usual channels do not.

How it runs

Theme intake and concept workshop. A creative lab cycle with your team. Production through the WildWooHoo studio and its collaborator network. Public release with you as named partner. Sized to your funding window, from a single piece to a multi-channel campaign.

Themes

Five themes · Drawn from the WildWooHoo studio practice

Each one runs as a sprint workshop, a creative lab, or a longer residency. Pick the depth that fits your funding window and reach goals.

T.01

Nature-science storytelling

Research translated into film, music, and visual story. Emotion-led, culturally fluent, designed for reach beyond academic channels.

T.02

Creative lab series

Multi-session experimentation for cross-disciplinary teams. Rapid testing, collaborative development, reusable methods you can run again later.

T.03

Transforming complex themes into compelling art

The Falling Walls Engage 2025 shortlisted workshop, adapted for institutions. Take a hard piece of research and shape it into work that lands with a specific audience without losing what made it worth doing.

T.04

Public-impact campaigns

Collaborations built for reach, co-created with institutions, festivals, and cultural partners through WildWooHoo.

T.05

Connecting science and culture in the creative space

Interconnected narratives across disciplines. A workshop for teams that want to see their work as part of a wider cultural conversation, and find the threads that link it to art, music, and public feeling.
